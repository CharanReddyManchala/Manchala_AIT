Introduction

 

Local Data for Better Health—a cooperation between the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation, the CDC Foundation, and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) whose goal is to offer high quality small area estimates for behavioral risk factors that influence health status, health outcomes, and the use of clinical preventive services. These figures can be used to spot emerging health issues and to plan and implement effective, focused public health prevention programs. Chronic diseases such as heart disease, asthma, and diabetes, as well as bad health behaviors such as inadequate physical exercise, are all issues that Americans face. However, there is a scarcity of data to adequately address the kind of health issues that communities and neighborhoods face.

 

Data set

This dataset contains county-level estimates based on models for the PLACES 2021 release. PLACES is a follow-up to the original 500 Cities Project, and it includes the whole United States—50 states plus the District of Columbia (DC)—at the counties, city, census tract, and ZIP Code Tabulation Area levels. It is the first time that information has been released systematically on such a big scale for local communities at four different geographic levels. The CDC's Division of Population Health, Epidemiology and Surveillance Branch provided the figures. Estimates for 18 variables are included in the dataset: chronic disease-related health risk behaviors, health outcomes, and health status. These data can be used to spot emerging health issues and to plan and implement effective, focused public health prevention programs.
